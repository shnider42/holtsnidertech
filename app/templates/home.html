Daily Flyer

A repeatable content engine for small, useful daily experiences.

Daily Flyer is a broader project idea: take structured source material, theme it, and publish it as a polished mini-page. It can support ideas like Irish Today, birthdays, local history, learning refreshers, team newsletters, or niche educational pages.

The long-term value is not one page. It is the pattern: repeatable content, clean presentation, flexible themes, and enough automation to make personalized or recurring pages practical.

Inputs Dates, themes, topics, people, places, and curated sources.
Engine Templates, data cleanup, rendering logic, and repeatable structure.
Outputs Simple web pages, themed flyers, portfolio demos, or client-ready prototypes.

Approach

Grounded, direct, and built around the actual problem.

How the work is approached

  • Start with the real issue, not just the software name attached to it.
  • Understand the business context before recommending tools.
  • Favor clarity, maintainability, and usefulness over hype.
  • Reduce complexity where possible instead of adding to it.
  • Keep communication understandable and direct.

Who this is for

  • Small businesses that need technical judgment without a full internal team.
  • People turning an idea, hobby, or workflow into a clearer public-facing project.
  • Operators dealing with aging systems, messy spreadsheets, or unclear tooling.
  • Clients who want someone who can troubleshoot and explain at the same time.

About Chris

Holtsnider Tech is built around practical judgment.

Holtsnider Tech reflects how Chris Holtsnider approaches technology: make the situation clearer, understand the constraints, and build the next useful thing instead of chasing complexity for its own sake.
